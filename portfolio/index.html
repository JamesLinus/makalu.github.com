What we’ve done

Over the past decade, we’ve had the opportunity to contribute to projects large and small, across many technological and application domains. Here are some highlights.

Day in the Cloud

One of our proudest moments was hearing Ogilvy executives refer to the game we created that anchored the Google & Virgin America “Day in the Cloud” campaign as, “An example of the future of marketing.” Back when Flash was the default interactive technology, we built this game in HTML, CSS and JavaScript, making it playable in the bandwidth contrained environment of airplane wifi at 30,000 feet.

ChessDrop

We built a personal chess database to record, analyze, and annotate your games. Read the blog article for a great insight into the interaction design process.

chessdrop.com →

Rego

Our place bookmarking iOS app took the world by storm when it launched in 2014. It was picked as one of the top apps by Apple and was featured on the App Store home page.

regoapp.com →

RaceSplitter

Our RaceSplitter iOS app for timing sports events has been used to time nearly 1.25 million participants in events in nearly 100 countries around the world.

racesplitter.com →

Catalog Choice

Engaged by a group of environmentalist from New York and California, we were hired to design, build and operate the original Catalog Choice site—a Rails app launched in 2007 that we scaled to support over one million members in its first year, and was covered on all major US media outlets.

Money for Something

Dipping our toes into self-publication, our co-founder wrote an introduction to investing that can be read in an hour, and was praised by people like Derek Sivers and David Heinemeier Hansson. To sell the book, we built a small Rails app that integrates with Gumroad.

moneyforsomethingbook.com →

Reinventing the investment calculator

In support of Matt’s investment book, Money for Something, we designed a new take on the interactive investment calculator, that received quite some attention.

Read about the design process →

Marek Lieberberg Konzertagentur

Over the course of a decade, we were the design and tech team for the Marek Lieberberg Konzertagentur, as they grew from startup to become the world’s fourth largest promoter, and ultimately being acquired by the world’s largest, Live Nation.

Rock am Ring Website

We created an innovative web app for Marek Lieberberg that from a single Rails code base drives three separate public festival websites and apps, servicing tens of millions of requests during the annual festival season.

rock-am-ring.com →

Rock am Ring iOS/Android App

To provide a consistent cross-platform user experience, we made extensive use of webviews in the MLK iOS and Android mobile festival apps, and relying on native technology for areas like venue mapping.

Multi-Festival CMS

To manage the ever-changing content and structure of a network of festival websites and mobile apps, across pre-festival, live and post-festival states, we created a unified and easy-to-use CMS for the Marek Lieberberg team.

Jawaker

Working with a team in Jordan, our interactive game design helped propel Jawaker to become the largest online card gaming site in the middle east.

The Henderson Brothers

Our website design for this north-Atlanta builder of luxury lake homes helped to convey the quality of product to be expected by the firms customers.

thehendersonbros.com →

Marek Lieberberg CMS

The CMS we developed and extended over a five year period helps the world’s fifth largest concert promoter market and collaborate on nearly 1,000 events per year across Germany, Austria and Switzerland.
